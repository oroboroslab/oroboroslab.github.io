Research Abstract

Building upon Volume I's temporal framework, this volume presents quantum mechanical evidence for reality's information-theoretic foundation. Drawing from experimental physics, the research examines how quantum coherence, entanglement, and measurement phenomena suggest an underlying substrate responsive to conscious observation.

The analysis synthesizes findings from double-slit experiments, quantum eraser studies, and delayed-choice protocols to construct a coherent ontological model. This model proposes that what we perceive as physical reality operates as an information-processing system with consciousness as an integral—not incidental—component.

All theoretical claims are grounded in published experimental data, with mathematical formalism provided for key propositions.
